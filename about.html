Liverpool is one of the best supported clubs in the world. The worldwide fan base includes more than 200 officially recognised Supporters Clubs in at least 50 countries. which has included playing in front of 101,000 in Michigan, U.S., and 95,000 in Melbourne, Australia. Liverpool fans often refer to themselves as Kopites, a reference to the fans who once stood, and now sit, on the Kop at Anfield.

The song "You'll Never Walk Alone", is the club's anthem and has been sung by the Anfield crowd since the early 1960s. The song's title adorns the top of the Shankly Gates, which were unveiled on 2 August 1982 in memory of former manager Bill Shankly. The "You'll Never Walk Alone" portion of the Shankly Gates is also reproduced on the club's crest.
